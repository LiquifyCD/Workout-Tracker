System rules
From the video
Principles
Progression
Recovery
StructureInfinite upper/lower loop — days 1–6, repeat continuously. No fixed rest day.
Sets per exercise1–2 hard sets max. Never 3+ for the same exercise in one session.
Weekly target~4–6 direct sets per joint action across the full 6-day cycle.
Priority methodDo the focus muscle FIRST in the session. Don't add extra sets to prioritize — order matters.
Arms always lastBiceps and triceps always trained last on upper days, unless they are a specific weak point.
Reps — compounds4–7 reps on stable presses, rows, pulldowns, squats, and deadlifts.
Reps — isolations6–10 for curls, extensions, pec deck, leg curl, leg extension.
Reps — raises/calves8–15 for lateral raises; 6–12 for calves.
Effort0–1 RIR on most sets. 1–2 RIR if joints feel irritated or performance is dropping.
Indirect workDo NOT count rows toward bicep volume, or presses toward tricep volume.
Muscle damageLow volume keeps damage low. High MPS ≠ growth. Myofibrillar protein synthesis is what matters.
Frequency logicMyops returns to baseline in ~48–72h. Atrophy begins at baseline. High frequency defeats atrophy constantly.
Use double progression. Example: machine shoulder press, 2 sets × 4–7 reps.

Increase whenBoth sets hit the TOP of the rep range AND RIR ≤ 1.
Repeat whenReps are within range but haven't maxed yet, or RIR was > 1 at top reps.
Reduce whenReps fall below the target minimum or RIR > 2 (can't actually hit the range).
Step size — barbell2.5–5 kg per increase.
Step size — machine/cable2.5 kg or one pin.
Step size — isolationsSmallest increment available. Form must stay clean before going up.
Take a rest day when any of these apply
Performance drops noticeably on two sessions in a row
Joints or tendons feel irritated or achy
Sleep has been poor for multiple nights
Motivation is unusually flat before you even warm up
You cannot hit normal loads without grinding
Pumps and contractions feel noticeably worse than usual
After restResume with the NEXT workout in the loop sequence. Don't skip ahead.
Mild fatigueKeep the plan but use 1–2 RIR instead of pushing to failure.
Moderate fatigueDrop optional sets (lateral raises, extra calf sets) but keep core lifts.
Strong fatigueTake one rest day. Resume next in sequence.
CNS noteLow volume = less muscle damage = fewer inflammatory signals to the brain = better recruitment every session.
